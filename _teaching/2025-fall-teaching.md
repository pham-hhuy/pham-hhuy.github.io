**Course Description:**

As though the syllabus may change from semester to semester, here are some of the notable topics we aim to cover in this course:

- Introduction to Inferential Statistics
    - Identifying populations, random samples, etc.
    - Parameters and Statistics
- Introduction to Probability
    - Basics of set theory in probability
    - Common probability rules
- Introduction to Combinatorics (permutations, combinations, etc.)
- Popular Statistical Distributions
    - Normal, Bernoulli, Poisson, Binomial, etc.
- Expected Value Properties
- Central Limit Theorem
    - Identifying CLT for sample mean and sample proportion    
- Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7)
- Estimating Parameters
    - Point estimators
    - Confidence Interval
- Hypothesis Testing
    - Sampling Distribution
    - One/Two sample(s) hypotheses testing
- Simple Linear Regression (special topic)

In addition to teaching these fundamental topics, we aim to provide an interactive learning environment by utilizing R and Rstudio. Relevant R coding exercises (mostly in base R) interacting with insightful data sets are provided throughout the semester.
